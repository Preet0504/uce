ID: RQ-005
Title: Users must have verified contact data
Priority: High
Description: Every record in the `users` table MUST include `email` and `email_verified`. The `email` field is the primary identity anchor for account recovery, authentication, and GDPR contact purposes. `email_verified` records whether the email address has been confirmed via a verification flow (e.g. token in inbox).
Acceptance Criteria:
  - `email` is unique, non-null, and validated against RFC 5322 format before storage.
  - `email_verified` defaults to `false` and is set to `true` only by the email-verification subsystem (not by direct UPDATE on the users table).
  - Unverified accounts are blocked from sensitive operations (e.g. payment, agent provisioning) until `email_verified = true`.
  - Re-verification is required when `email` changes (the flag resets to `false`).
Tags: authentication, identity, contact
GDPR Note: `email` is a direct personal identifier under Art. 4(1) and has high sensitivity. It must be included in Art. 17 erasure requests and Art. 20 portability exports. The verification timestamp should also be stored for Art. 7 consent-record purposes.
