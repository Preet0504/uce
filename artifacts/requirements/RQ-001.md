ID: RQ-001
Title: Store core meeting status and timestamps
Priority: High
Description: Every record in the `meetings` table MUST include `status`, `created_at`, and `updated_at`. These fields are required to track meeting lifecycle events (e.g. scheduled → active → completed → cancelled) and to produce an immutable audit trail of state changes.
Acceptance Criteria:
  - `status` is a non-null enumerated field with at least the values: scheduled, active, completed, cancelled.
  - `created_at` is set once on INSERT and never overwritten.
  - `updated_at` is automatically refreshed on every UPDATE via a trigger or ORM lifecycle hook.
  - An audit log entry (table or event stream) captures who changed `status` and at what time.
Tags: audit, lifecycle, meetings
GDPR Note: `created_at` and `updated_at` are activity timestamps linked to the meeting owner (user_id). They constitute behavioural personal data under Art. 4(1) and must be included in any Art. 17 erasure response.
